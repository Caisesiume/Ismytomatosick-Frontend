Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)

One of the most devastating plant diseases of all time. Can develop quickly at any time of the crop

Early Blight (Alternaria solani)

One of the most common tomato plant diseases. Stem infections easily spread and infect the fruit.

Septoria Leaf Spot (Septoria lycopersici)

A common disease for plants. Spreads by fungus spores that can travel great distance

Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (Begomovirus)

The most destructive disease of tomato. Commonly to be found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Bacterial Spot (Xanthomonas vesicatoria)

A potentially devastating disease that can lead to unmarketable fruit and even plant death.

Target Spot (Corynespora cassiicola)

Not to be confused with bacterial spot and early blight. Plants are most susceptible just before fruiting.

Tomato Mosaic Virus (Tobamovirus)

Causes severe stunting, distortion and necrosis with a diffuse chlorotic leaf spotting. Difficult to treat.

Leaf Mold (Cladosporium fulvum)

Common disease after longer periods of high humidity. Seen primarily in hoophouses and greenhouses.

Two‑Spotted Spider Mites (Tetranychus urticae)

Causes serious damage to crops during hot dry weather. Mites injures tomatoes and lives on the leafs.

Powdery Mildew (Tetranychus urticae)

An important disease problem worldwide. The disease mainly affects leaves causing drying.
